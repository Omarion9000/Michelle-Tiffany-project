About the Tiffany Diamond

The Tiffany Diamond is one of the largest and finest yellow diamonds in the world, weighing 128.54 carats. It was discovered in the Kimberley diamond mines in South Africa in 1877 and acquired by Charles Lewis Tiffany, the founder of Tiffany & Co.

Its remarkable size, stunning color, and exquisite craftsmanship have made it one of the most iconic gemstones in history. The diamond has been worn by only a select few, including Audrey Hepburn during the promotional tour for "Breakfast at Tiffany's."

Specifications

Weight: 128.54 carats

Color: Fancy Yellow

Shape: Cushion-cut

Clarity: Internally Flawless
